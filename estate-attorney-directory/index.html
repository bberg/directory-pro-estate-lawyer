Frequently Asked Questions

What is an estate attorney?

An estate attorney helps individuals and families plan for the management and transfer of their assets. This includes drafting wills and trusts, planning to minimize estate taxes, arranging powers of attorney, and guiding families through probate after a death. Some estate attorneys also specialize in elder law and Medicaid planning.

How do I find an estate planning attorney near me?

Use the state directories above to find firms practicing in your area, then narrow by specialty. Each listing describes the firm's focus areas and location so you can identify attorneys who handle your specific needs, whether that is a simple will, a complex trust, or probate administration.

What is the difference between estate planning and probate?

Estate planning is the proactive process of preparing documents like wills and trusts while you are alive. Probate is the court-supervised process of validating a will and distributing assets after death. Many firms in this directory handle both.

Are the firms in this directory vetted?

Every firm listed has a detailed profile describing its real practice areas and location. We focus on listing genuine, established firms so the directory stays useful. Always confirm credentials, experience, and fees directly with any firm before hiring.
