## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Texas require probate?
Texas has a probate process, but it is often more streamlined than in other states, especially with independent administration. Estate plans using trusts and beneficiary designations can reduce or avoid probate. Firms like Texas Trust Law focus on probate-avoidance planning.

### Is there an estate tax in Texas?
Texas has no state estate or inheritance tax. However, large estates may still be subject to the federal estate tax, which is where tax-focused firms like Chamberlain Hrdlicka can help.

### How much does an estate plan cost in Texas?
Costs vary by complexity, from a few hundred dollars for a basic will to several thousand for a comprehensive trust-based plan. Contact firms directly for quotes.

### Do I need a lawyer for a will in Texas?
You are not legally required to use a lawyer, but an attorney helps ensure your will is valid, clear, and tailored to Texas law, reducing the risk of disputes and probate complications.
