## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does New Jersey have an estate or inheritance tax?
New Jersey repealed its estate tax, but it still imposes an inheritance tax on transfers to certain beneficiaries (such as more distant relatives and non-relatives). An estate planning attorney can help structure your plan to minimize this tax.

### Is probate required in New Jersey?
New Jersey probate is generally simpler than in many states, but it is still required to administer assets held solely in the decedent's name. Trusts and beneficiary designations can reduce probate.

### What documents should a New Jersey estate plan include?
A typical plan includes a will, a durable power of attorney, and an advance healthcare directive, and often a trust. Both firms above can prepare a complete plan tailored to your needs.

### How do I choose a New Jersey estate planning attorney?
Compare firms by focus area and experience, then contact a few for consultations to discuss your goals and fees.
