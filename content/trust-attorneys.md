## Types of Trusts a Trust Attorney Can Set Up

1. **Revocable living trusts** - Flexible trusts you can change during your lifetime that help avoid probate.
2. **Irrevocable trusts** - Trusts that remove assets from your taxable estate for tax savings and protection.
3. **Charitable trusts** - Vehicles that support causes you care about while offering tax benefits.
4. **Special needs trusts** - Trusts that protect benefits eligibility for disabled beneficiaries.
5. **Asset protection trusts** - Trusts, such as Nevada Asset Protection Trusts, designed to shield assets from creditors.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does a trust attorney do?
A trust attorney advises you on which type of trust fits your goals, drafts the trust documents, and helps fund and administer the trust. They also assist trustees with their duties and can handle trust disputes and litigation.

### Do I need a trust or just a will?
It depends on your goals. A will directs how assets are distributed but still goes through probate. A trust can avoid probate, provide privacy, and offer ongoing management of assets. Many people use both. A trust attorney can help you decide.

### What is an asset protection trust?
An asset protection trust is an irrevocable trust designed to shield assets from future creditors. Some states, like Nevada, have favorable laws for these trusts. Firms such as Lee Kiefer Park specialize in Nevada Asset Protection Trusts.

### How much does it cost to set up a trust?
Costs vary by complexity and firm, ranging from a flat fee for a basic revocable trust to higher fees for complex irrevocable or tax-driven trusts. Contact firms directly for quotes.
