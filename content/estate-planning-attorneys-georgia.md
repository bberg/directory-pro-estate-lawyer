## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Georgia have an estate tax?
Georgia has no state estate or inheritance tax. Large estates may still be subject to the federal estate tax.

### Is probate required in Georgia?
Georgia requires probate to administer assets held solely in the decedent's name, handled through the county Probate Court. Some estates qualify for simplified procedures, and trusts can help avoid probate.

### What is elder law and do I need it in Georgia?
Elder law covers legal issues affecting seniors, including Medicaid planning, guardianship, and long-term care. If you or a loved one is aging or has special needs, a firm like The Law Office of Paul Black can help.

### How do I choose a Georgia estate planning attorney?
Match the firm's focus (elder law, tax, probate) to your needs, then contact a few for consultations to discuss your goals and fees.
