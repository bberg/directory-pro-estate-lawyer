## What Probate Lawyers Do

A probate attorney can help executors and families with:

1. **Filing the estate** - Submitting the will and opening the probate case with the court.
2. **Asset inventory** - Identifying, valuing, and managing estate assets.
3. **Paying debts and taxes** - Settling creditor claims and filing final tax returns.
4. **Distributing assets** - Transferring property to beneficiaries per the will or state law.
5. **Resolving disputes** - Handling will contests and litigation when conflicts arise.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a probate lawyer?
Not every estate requires an attorney, but probate lawyers are valuable when the estate is large, the will is contested, assets are complex, or family disputes arise. Many firms in this directory offer consultations to help you decide whether you need full representation or limited guidance.

### How long does probate take?
Probate timelines vary by state and estate complexity, often ranging from several months to more than a year. A probate attorney can help streamline the process and avoid common delays.

### Can probate be avoided?
Yes. Tools like revocable living trusts, beneficiary designations, and joint ownership can keep assets out of probate. Many firms here, such as CunninghamLegal and Texas Trust Law, focus on probate-avoidance planning. See our [trust attorney directory](/trust-attorneys) for more.

### How much do probate lawyers cost?
Fees vary by state and firm and may be hourly, flat-fee, or a percentage of the estate. Contact firms directly for fee structures specific to your situation.
